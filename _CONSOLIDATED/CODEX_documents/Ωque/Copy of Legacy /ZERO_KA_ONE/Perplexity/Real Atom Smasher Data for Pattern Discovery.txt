## Where to Find Real Atom Smasher Data

- **CERN Open Data Portal**: The main source for open-access, real-collision datasets from LHC experiments. Data covers proton-proton collisions, heavy-ion events, and includes documentation and analysis tools usable by researchers, students, and independent teams[1][2][3][4][5].
- **CMS and LHCb Experiments**: Both collaborations have made detailed “primary” and “derived” datasets publicly available, covering entire runs with hundreds of terabytes of data. These datasets are accompanied by open source software and example code[3][5].
- **Educational Access**: Smaller “derived” datasets are especially suitable for advanced non-specialist explorations, including data-driven pattern hunting without the need for heavy computing infrastructure[3][5][4].
